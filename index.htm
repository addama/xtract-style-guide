#Use of Color

Keep It Professional

Keep your use of color to a minimum, using it to distinguish important elements, separate similar elements, or assert brand identity. The majority of your documents, sites, etc, should be white, black and/or dark gray.

Flat Is Timeless

Avoid patterns or backgrounds whenever possible, except in solely decorative areas. Prefer flat, solid color backgrounds using neutral colors, preferrably white.

Use Common Sense

Use obvious colors to signal status to the user. Red is bad, and signifies errors, dangerous actions, and warnings. Green is good, and signifies acceptance, default actions, and good decisions. Yellow signifies caution, and alternative actions; meanwhile, Blue is an alternative to Green if more than one acceptable action is present. Use Gray to present actions and messages that the user needs or can do, but they aren't important or common.

If you have access to a style library in code (e.g. Bootstrap), use its default color scheme for buttons, as it will likely follow these rules without you having to determine your own color palette.

Fly The Colors

Use only Xtract Blue and Xtract Yellow and neutral colors in company letters, correspondence, manuals, and forms. These colors represent our brand, and no one should be confused about that.

The legacy of our Gen 1 products gave us a wide selection of colors, each specific to that product. While these are legacy products, and they and their colors won't be seen too much anymore, their colors are a part of our heritage and should be preserved.
